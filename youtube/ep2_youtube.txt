High inflation and high unemployment at the same time. The textbooks said it was impossible.

The 1970s stagflation crisis shattered the Keynesian consensus that had shaped economic policy since the Depression. Oil shocks, wage-price spirals, and the limits of demand management combined to produce a decade of economic stagnation that reshaped the entire field of macroeconomics. The solution — Volcker's brutal interest rate shock — ended inflation but at the cost of the deepest recession since the 1930s.
