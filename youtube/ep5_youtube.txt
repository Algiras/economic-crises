The fastest economic contraction in modern history — and the most rapid policy response ever deployed.

The COVID-19 economic shock of 2020 was unlike any previous crisis: deliberately induced, simultaneous across all sectors, and met with an unprecedented speed of fiscal and monetary response. The lessons drawn from 2008 meant governments acted in weeks rather than years — but also planted the seeds of the inflation crisis that followed.
