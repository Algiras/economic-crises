Inflation hadn't been a serious problem for 30 years. Then, almost everywhere at once, it came back.

The 2021–2023 inflation surge caught most central banks off guard — dismissed initially as transitory, it proved durable and broad-based. Supply chain disruption, energy price shocks, and the delayed effects of pandemic stimulus combined to produce the most aggressive rate-tightening cycle in a generation.
