Every major economic crisis of the past century was, in the months before it happened, declared impossible by the people responsible for preventing it.

This series examines seventeen economic crises spanning three centuries — what happened, why the tools of the time failed, what was done, and what changed permanently. Each episode follows the same structure: the trigger events, the quantitative record, the human cost, the policy response, and the honest accounting of what was learned. The argument running through all of them is the same: before we can manage economies better, we need to understand more clearly why managing them is hard.
