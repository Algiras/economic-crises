In September 2008, the global financial system came within hours of complete collapse.

The 2008 Global Financial Crisis was the most severe economic shock since the Great Depression. Originating in the US mortgage market, it spread through complex financial instruments to bring down major institutions worldwide. The policy response — unprecedented in scale — prevented a depression but left a decade of slow recovery and rising inequality.
