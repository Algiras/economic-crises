Nine thousand banks failed. Twenty-five percent unemployment. And the policies meant to stabilise the economy made it worse.

The Great Depression was not just a stock market crash — it was a systemic collapse that exposed every flaw in the relationship between governments and markets. This episode traces how the crash of 1929 triggered a cascade of bank failures, deflation, and global contagion that no existing policy framework could handle.
